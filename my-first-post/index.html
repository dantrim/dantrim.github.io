Mathematical Formula

The information entropy, $ H $, over a discrete data is given as, $$ H = - \sum_i p_i \cdot log_2 (p_i), $$ where the $ p_i $ are the probabilities of codes/states, indexed by $ i $, in the data.

When considering a continuous distribution, interpreted as a probability distribution function that satisfies $ \int_{-\inf}^{+\inf} p(x) dx = 1$, the information entropy is given by,

$$ H(x) = - \int_{-\inf}^{+\inf} p(x) \cdot log_2[p(x)] dx. $$
